Discovery Day 2026

The University of Manchester is on a mission to build one of the UK's most intelligent, sustainable, and inclusive campuses. We're already investing in new infrastructure, accessibility programmes, and green energy targets — and we want to use technology to go further.

Today you'll be split into five teams, each given a piece of cutting-edge sensing technology. Your challenge: explore how your tool could contribute to a smarter campus — then pitch your idea by placing your solution on a live map of the university.

At the end, all five solutions need to work together as one unified platform. You're not just building a feature — you're designing the future of this campus.

Your Challenge — Smart Energy

The University has committed to reaching net zero carbon emissions by 2038. As part of that plan, we want to create a thermal map of our estate — understanding exactly where targeted insulation and glazing improvements would have the biggest impact, and making that data visible to students and facilities teams alike.

Your thermal camera captures surface temperature variation with millimetre precision, even at night. How would you design a survey system, and what would you do with the data it generates?

Workshop Finale — 7 minutes
When machines learn to listen
One more student is going to show you something they built as a side project. It combines two things you've probably heard of — but haven't seen working together quite like this.
🖼️
Image Recognition
A model trained to identify and classify handshapes and movements from video — watching the camera 30 times per second and labelling what it sees in real time.
💬
Natural Language Processing
A language model that takes the sequence of recognised signs and constructs coherent English sentences — understanding grammar and context, not just individual gestures.
The Result
A system that can watch someone signing in American Sign Language and translate it into written English in real time — with no human interpreter needed. Built by a student, not a research lab, during their second year here.
The bigger point
Today you used thermal cameras, drones, and mixed reality headsets to reimagine this campus. Then you connected them into a single system. Then a student showed you a tool that gives people back their voice. The tools change. The mindset — "what problem can I solve with what I have?" — stays the same. That's what we do here.
